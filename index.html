Abstract

We propose Compact and Swift Segmenting 3D Gaussians(CoSSegGaussians), a method for compact 3D-consistent scene segmentation at fast rendering speed with only RGB images input. Previous NeRF-based 3D segmentation methods have relied on implicit or voxel neural scene representation and ray-marching volume rendering which are time consuming. Recent 3D Gaussian Splatting significantly improves the rendering speed, however, existing Gaussian-based segmentation methods(eg: Gaussian Grouping), struggle to produce compact masks, especially in zero-shot segmentation, which stems from the lack of robustness when encountering inconsistent 2D machine-generated labels for their straightforward assignment of learnable parameters to each Gaussian. Our method aims to alleviate this problem by mapping semantically meaningful priors fused with spatial features to compact segmenting features for each Gaussian point with a shallow decoding network. Specifically, our method firstly optimizes 3D Gaussians under the supervision of RGB images. After Gaussian Locating, we distill multi-scale DINO features extracted from images through unprojection to each Gaussian, which is further incorporated with spatial features from the fast point features processing network, i.e. RandLA-Net. Then the decoding MLP is applied to the multi-scale fused features to obtain compact segmentation. Experimental results show that our model can perform high-quality zero-shot scene segmentation, as our model outperforms baselines on both semantic and panoptic segmentation task,meanwhile consumes approximately only 10% segmenting time compared to NeRF-based segmentation.

Methods

Given only posed RGB images of a 3D scene, our method aims to build an expressive representation to capture appearance, geometry as well as compact segmenting identity of the scene. Our proposed method, CoSSegGaussians, enables compact novel-view 3D-consistent scene segmentation, while consuming much less rendering time compared to NeRF-based segmentation methods.
